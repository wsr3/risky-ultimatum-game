Title of Study: The Study of Bargaining Game

Investigators:  Shirui Wang and Elizabeth Hoffman

This form describes a research project. It has information to help you decide whether or not you wish to participate. Research studies include only people who choose to take part—your participation is completely voluntary. Please discuss any questions you have about the study or about this form with the project staff before deciding to participate. 

Introduction

The purpose of this study is to better understand the bargaining behaviors under various conditions. You are being invited to participate in this study because you showed an interest in this study. You should not participate if you are under age 18.

Description of Procedures

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to visit the website at the time slot that you choose. This will be an interactive website in which you will play a bargaining game. No extensions will be downloaded or installed. You won’t be asked to accept cookies either.

During the game, you will be randomly paired with other anonymous participants for several rounds, each round with a new participant. The game will involve making/accepting offers to share some money. Your final compensation will be determined by your choices in each round of the game.

Then you will answer a questionnaire regarding demographic characteristics and risk preference. Your participation will last for 20 minutes approximately.

Risks or Discomforts

There are no foreseeable risks at this time in participating in the study.

Benefits

If you decide to participate in this study, there will be no direct benefit to you. It is hoped that the information gained in this study will benefit society by providing more insight into the process of how people make decisions.

Costs and Compensation

You will not have any costs from participating in this study. You will be compensated for participating in this study. The compensation varies from 0 to 7 dollars depending on your decisions. You can quit the game any time, but to receive payment you will need to complete the whole game and answer follow-up questions, some of which can be skipped if you don’t want to answer.

Participant Rights

Participating in this study is completely voluntary. You may choose not to take part in the study or to stop participating at any time, for any reason, without penalty or negative consequences. You can skip any questions that you do not wish to answer.

 

If you have any questions about the rights of research subjects or research-related injury, please contact the IRB Administrator, (515) 294-4566, IRB@iastate.edu, or Director, (515) 294-3115, Office for Responsible Research, Iowa State University, Ames, Iowa 50011.

Confidentiality

The investigators will not have access to your information linking to Amazon Mechanical Turk. The raw data will be processed to exclude names, and then stored with encryption. All observations will be identified by random ID numbers in the processed data. The data can only be accessed by the investigators and will be stored on computers with password protected.

 

Records identifying participants will be kept confidential to the extent permitted by applicable laws and regulations and will not be made publicly available. However, federal government regulatory agencies, auditing departments of Iowa State University, and the Institutional Review Board (a committee that reviews and approves human subject research studies) may inspect and/or copy study records for quality assurance and data analysis. These records may contain private information.

Questions

You are encouraged to ask questions at any time during this study. For further information about the study, contact Shirui Wang at (515) 294-8288, 82 Heady Hall, wsr@iastate.edu; or Elizabeth Hoffman at bhoffman@iastate.edu.
